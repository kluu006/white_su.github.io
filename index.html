Effects of White Supremacy in America

By Kenny Luu and Balmeet Singh

What is White Supremacy?

A system of power that drives the nation by providing a foundation for its racial and social hierarchies.

Three Pillars of White Supremacy

  • Slavery/Capitalism
  • Genocide/Colonialism
  • Orientalism/War

Slavery/Capitalism

Blackness becomes equated with slaveability. This creates a racial hierarchy that defines the foundation of what White Supremacy stands on.

Genocide/Colonialism

Genocide serves as the groundwork for colonialism and its expansion in order to "rightfully own" indigenous people's lands.

Orientalism/War

... The process of the West defining itself as a superior civilization by constructing itself in opposition to an "exotic" but inferior "Orient" -Edward Said

Orientalism is used as justification for war to protect itself from its enemies.

Columbus arrives at the West Indies, 1492

Use the Space key to navigate through all slides.

He mistakenly thought that the land he set foot on was India, so the land was named the West Indies.

Genocide of the Hispaniola

After he arrived, he claimed the land by committing genocide of the indigenous people, the Hispanoila.

The Beginning effects of White Supremacy

This initiated the practice of colonialism in the Americas. Eventually leading into the three pillars of White Supremacy becoming common sense.

Declaration of Independence, 1776

Documents that symbolize freedom that states all men are created equal and are entitled to inalienable rights.

(Unless you are non-white, not free, not a man, and not a property owner)

Independence...

Set into motion the American revolution and ultimately founding the United States as a nation state.

and Beyond

Gave the United States a moral justification for the perpetuation of White Supremacy in America and the rest of the world.

Manifest Destiny

The idea that the Anglo-Saxons, according to the word of God, had the right to rule over any race for the prosperity of the nation as a whole.


With the manifest destiny, the United States started to expand towards the West starting with the Louisiana purchase.

Native Americans

They were either forced to assimilate, be expelled from their lands, or had genocide be brought upon them. The Trail of Tears was an example of how the Native Americans were affected by White Supremacy under the justification of the Manifest Destiny.

Settler Colonialism

The perpetuation of settler colonialism replacing the histories of the indigenous people. With this, the Anglo-Saxons claimed rights to the lands which is present day United States.

Slavery, 1776-1860

The enslavement of primarily Africans that were imported for the sake of free labor. They justified this through the concept of a racial hierarchy stemmed from the White Supremacy power structures.


Abolishment of Slavery

Led to the arrival of about 600,000 African slaves to the US before slavery was abolished. The dehumanization of slaves from laws such as the Three-Fifths Compromise.

Racial Hierarchy

Began the racial hierarchy in America that has sustained itself since that time. Blacks were set at the bottom of the hierarchy and were used as a baseline for White Supremacy to survive.

Civil War and Aftermath 1860-1865

A war between the Northern Union and the Southern Confederacy that ended with the abolishment of slavery and the change of the economic focus of the United States.

The Thirteenth Amendment

The thirteenth amendment was passed that ended slavery and allowed Blacks to vote. Even with slavery gone, the system not end the oppression of the former slaves due to the existing prejudices against them. Minorities faced poverty from unfair work conditions, while Whites are better off.

White Supremacy's Appearance

Nothing really changed because the appearance of White Supremacy just shifted from slavery to other forms of oppression.

Jim Crow/Racial Segregation 1877-1965

The legalization of racial segregation in both the Southern and Northern United States. It was the upheld by court cases such as Plessy vs. Ferguson which stated that accommodations must be separate but equal.

Discrimination

Discrimination and the blatant lack of equality that legalized the racial hierarchy that put Black at the bottom and Whites at the top. Also set up how the social hierarchy where Blacks were given less opportunities as Whites

Social Hierarchy

This period is the reason that the social hierarchy is the way it is today where the Whites were able to capitalize on the advantages gained throughout history. This was also the next manifestation of White Supremacy in the United States.

New Deal and GI Bill

Social programs that were implemented to help certain peoples and to support them. The GI bill was specifically for veterans.

The Gap Between Whites and People of Color

The bills assisted many Americans, but was administered in a racially discriminatory fashion. This widened the gap between Whites and the people of color who did not benefit from these social programs.

Differences Between People

This once again led to a larger differential between the races in a social hierarchy. More advantages were gained for Whites over people of color through opportunities given by government organizations.

Civil Rights Act 1964

Added legislation that gave basic rights to minorities. The Civil Rights Act prohibited discrimination on color, race, and religion.

The End of Segregation

Certain legislation such as the Brown vs. Board of Education was deemed unconstitutional. This ended segregation which allowed minorities to ideally have the opportunities as the Whites.

Racial Tensions

Though segregation has ended, racial tensions are still high. More and more activists start to rise up in order to push for equality or better treatment of minorities
